Utah

Utah is located on the western side of the United States. Its name was derived from the Ute Indians who inhabited the land. Utah received its statehood in 1896 as the 45th state but was settled by Brigham Young in 1847 by the first group of Mormon Pioneer who were fleeing religious persecution in the state of Illinois. To this day, over half of the population of the state identifies as being members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The state is also often referred to as the 'Beehive State'. This symbol references the state's perseverance and industry.
